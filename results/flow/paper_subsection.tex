\subsection{Mechanistic Case Study: Where Ambiguity Lives in the Network}
\label{sec:mechanistic}

The behavioral results (§\ref{sec:exp}) show that frontier models default to the naive
interpretation rather than clarifying. A natural question is whether the bottleneck is
\emph{recognition} (the model never internally registers the ambiguity) or \emph{action} (it
registers the ambiguity but answers anyway). Because this requires access to hidden states, we
run a small mechanistic probe on two open models as a proxy: \textbf{Qwen3-4B-Instruct}
(dense, 37 layers) and \textbf{Qwen3.5-4B} (hybrid linear/full attention, 33 layers).

\paragraph{Method.} For each instance we form a within-item contrast: the bare ambiguous
question $Q$ versus $Q$ concatenated with its disambiguating context $C$. We define a per-layer
\emph{ambiguity direction} as the difference in mean final-token activations between the two
conditions, and score any hidden state by its (mean-centered) projection onto that direction.
Tracing this score across layers gives a depth profile of how strongly each layer separates an
under-specified question from a disambiguated one. An analogous entity$-$metric direction
measures at which depth the model distinguishes the \emph{type} of ambiguity.

\paragraph{Result.} Figure~\ref{fig:crossmodel} shows the profiles overlaid for both models
(normalized for differing depth and scale). The signal is near-zero through the first
$\sim$60\% of layers and rises sharply to a peak in the \emph{final few layers}
(layer 35/37 and 32/33 respectively, $\approx$95--97\% relative depth). The entity-scope and
metric-definition directions separate with \emph{opposite sign} at the same peak layer in both
models. Despite different attention mechanisms, layer counts, and library versions, the two
architectures agree: financial-query ambiguity, and its axis, is linearly encoded late in the
stack.

\paragraph{Finding 6: Open models linearly represent both ambiguity and its axis in their final
layers, yet still default rather than clarify.} The internal representation exists and is
type-specific, replicating across two distinct architectures; the behavioral gap is therefore
better read as a failure to \emph{act} on a represented ambiguity than a failure to register it.

\paragraph{Scope and caveats.} This is mechanistic evidence on open proxies, not the closed
frontier models benchmarked in §\ref{sec:exp}; diff-in-means probing establishes linear
decodability, not causal use; and axis-type analysis is statistically supported only for the two
well-populated axes (entity\_scope, metric\_definition). We deliberately do \emph{not} draw
behavioral conclusions (e.g.\ clarification rates) from these open models' generations, as
surface cues confound them; the asking-behavior claims come solely from the graded closed-model
evaluation in §\ref{sec:exp}.
